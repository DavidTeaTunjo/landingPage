Lacto-Fermented Processing

Fermentation guided by lactic acid bacteria yields creamy acidity and layered complexity.

  • Select high-brix cherries using IoT-connected refractometers.
  • Disinfect and place depulped beans in sealed anaerobic tanks.
  • Inoculate previously isolated cultures of Lactobacillae.
  • Track pH, Temperature and other metrics using IoT fermentation controllers.
  • Ferment for about 72 hours, or until completion, guided by sensor alerts.
  • Dry on beds with constant humidity and temperature monitoring.
  • Evaluate results with cupping profiles and batch traceability.

Washed Processing

Cherries are depulped and fermented to highlight clarity, acidity, and terroir-specific flavor.

  • Use IoT tools to schedule optimal harvest based on sugar content.
  • Depulp cherries using calibrated mechanical depulpers.
  • Ferment in tanks for 12–24 hours, tracking pH and temperature with IoT sensors.
  • Wash beans with clean water until mucilage is removed.
  • Dry beans on beds or patios, adjusting exposure with sensor data.
  • Monitor moisture content continuously with IoT probes.
  • Store in breathable bags in monitored warehouses.

Natural Processing

Cherries are dried whole under controlled conditions to enhance fruit-forward flavors and complexity.

  • Harvest only ripe cherries, using IoT sensors to track ripeness indicators.
  • Spread cherries on raised beds in sun-rich, ventilated areas.
  • Use IoT-based drying monitors to track moisture and ambient conditions.
  • Turn cherries regularly for uniform drying.
  • Adjust shade levels based on IoT alerts for humidity or temperature spikes.
  • Dry to 10-12% moisture content over 21–30 days.
  • Store in climate-monitored spaces with real-time monitoring.

Honey Processing

Cherries are depulped and dried with mucilage to develop a sweet, balanced profile.

  • Harvest ripe cherries using maturity data from field IoT sensors.
  • Depulp carefully to retain desired mucilage level.
  • Dry beans on raised beds under partial shade.
  • Use IoT-controlled dryers or alert systems to stabilize conditions.
  • Turn beans frequently to prevent over-fermentation.
  • Target moisture content of 10.5–11.5%.
  • Track drying time and consistency with real-time data logs.

Double Alcoholic Fermentation

Two-phase fermentation intensifies flavor, structure, and aromatic complexity in premium lots.

  • Ferment whole cherries anaerobically for 48 hours, monitoring yeast activity with IoT tools.
  • Depulp and transfer to new tanks with mucilage and farm-specific isolated yeasts for second fermentation.
  • Continue fermentation for 60 hours, tracking sugar breakdown with real-time analytics.
  • Stabilize microbial activity through IoT-driven pH and ORP sensors.
  • Dry beans slowly on beds, adjusting based on ambient conditions via weather-linked data.
  • Ensure target moisture content using IoT moisture sensors.
  • Archive process data and batch history to support quality validation and export compliance.
